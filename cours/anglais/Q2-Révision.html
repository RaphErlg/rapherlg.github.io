🟪 PARTIE 2 · CONTENT of the 4 units (contenu)

💡 Key concepts you must be able to explain in English. (Concepts clés à savoir expliquer. Réponses détaillées des exercices : notes Unit-1 à Unit4.) 💬 Contenu en anglais, traduction française en italique.

📱 Unit 1 — Digital devices

  • An IT system = hardware + software used for a specific purpose. — Matériel + logiciel pour un but précis.

  • A bakery uses one single IT system with different computers; a car company uses several IT systems in a large network. — Boulangerie : un seul système. Constructeur auto : plusieurs systèmes en réseau.

  • Advantages of a laptop: cheaper, portable, usable without a power supply, built-in peripherals, mobile. — Moins cher, portable, sans alimentation, périphériques intégrés.

  • The most common network is client-server. Servers can be very large because they store a lot of data. — Le réseau le plus courant ; serveurs grands car ils stockent beaucoup de données.

  • A set-top box has two-way communication (interactive: add channels, vote…). — Décodeur : communication bidirectionnelle.

  • Digital technology can disrupt whole industries (Spotify ↔ music media · Uber ↔ classic taxis). — Peut bouleverser des industries entières.

📶 Unit 2 — Connectivity

  • Wired: faster, more stable, more secure — but needs physical access, less convenient → best for sensitive data. — Plus rapide/stable/sûr, mais accès physique requis.

  • Wireless: freedom of movement, no cable — but unstable (interference) and vulnerable to hacking → secure it with WPA/WPA2 and change default passwords. — Liberté de mouvement, mais instable et piratable.

  • Fibre vs copper: fibre has a higher throughput (so it seems faster); it uses pulses of light vs electrical pulses. Copper is already installed, cheaper short-term, conducts electricity, but overheats / causes interference. — La fibre a un débit plus élevé ; le cuivre est déjà là et moins cher.

  • Cellular: a cell (area) + a transmitting tower (antenna) → ISP → internet. Signals are blocked by obstacles (hills, walls, wired glass). In towns, small antennas + waves bouncing off buildings help. — Une cellule + une antenne relais ; signaux bloqués par les obstacles.

  • Digital divide = the gap between people who have internet and those who don't. — La fracture numérique.

🌐 Unit 3 — Types of network

  • A modem connects your home to the internet; a router broadcasts the Wi-Fi around your home. — Le modem connecte ; le routeur diffuse le Wi-Fi.

  • Leased line: very fast (up to 10 Gbps), not shared, secure — but expensive and ~3 months to install. — Très rapide, dédiée, mais chère et longue à installer.

  • VPN: a pair of routers creating a secure tunnel. — Paire de routeurs, tunnel sécurisé.

    • Corporate VPN: for a company, needs a username + password, cheaper than a leased line. — Pour l'entreprise ; username + password.

    • Personal VPN: protects an individual on public Wi-Fi; uses the company's IP → anonymity. — Protège l'individu sur Wi-Fi public ; anonymat.

  • The Great Firewall (China): censorship; it blocks Google/BBC/WhatsApp, uses ~50,000 censors, aims for cyber sovereignty. Free VPNs are dangerous (they harvest data). — Censure chinoise ; les VPN gratuits récoltent les données.

🚀 Unit 4 — New technologies

  • Evolution: devices have become much smaller and more powerful (what we hold in our hand would have been a supercomputer a few decades ago). — Appareils plus petits et puissants.

  • Benefits vs risks: AI and neural interfaces (treating chronic pain) bring benefits, but cybersecurity is a risk in a connected world. — Bénéfices (IA, interfaces neuronales) mais risque cybersécurité.

  • Neural interfaces: devices that interact with the nervous system; they could replace drug treatments and enhance human abilities. — Interagissent avec le système nerveux.

  • Quantum computing: solves in seconds calculations that would take thousands of years; Michelle Simmons (Australian of the Year 2018); large-scale commercialization is still far off. — Résout en secondes des calculs énormes.

  • Past vs present: floppy disk → thumb drive · book → Kindle · TV → UHD TV · mobile phone → smartphone · ENIAC → pocket calculator.
